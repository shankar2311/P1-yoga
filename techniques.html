Eight types of Yoga

Ashtanga Yoga

Ashtanga is a dynamic form of yoga and is often described as one of the most physically demanding forms of yoga. If you are looking for yoga for anxiety and stress relief, upper back pain, lower back pain, or fat loss, ashtanga would be the best for you.

Aerial Yoga

Aerial yoga helps to build toned and lean muscles. It has the goodness of yoga and cardio exercises like swimming or running. In this particular yoga, a rope suspends our body in the air, and poses are introduced hereafter.

Kundalini Yoga

Also known as the “yoga of awareness” includes repetitive poses and also suitable for stress, anxiety, depression. Movements begin with a chant, followed by pranayama, the act of controlled and precise breathing.

Restorative Yoga

Restorative yoga aims to relax completely into the various poses. The main focus lies in relaxing the nervous system. A great form to actively destress and calm those anxious nerves. The idea is to hold poses for a few minutes.

Hatha Yoga

Hatha means “force.” Which restores the balance of the body. It comprises many physical postures and positions that balance the body and mind. Research shows that Hatha yoga helps us destress, stay physically fit and agile, and make us look younger.

Iyengar Yoga

Iyengar yoga focuses primarily on posture, body alignment and opening of the body. It also uses props such as yoga blocks and belts to perfect poses. This type of yoga is quite similar to vinyasa yoga.

Power Yoga

Power yoga derives its roots from ashtanga yoga. one needs to perform Yoga postures at a much quicker pace. It works to tone the muscles and also enhances flexibility. It is also an excellent form of yoga for burning calories.

Vinyasa Yoga

This form of yoga is also called “flow” yoga. It creates a flow of postures with controlled breathing replicating the flow of life. Meditation is a part of vinyasa yoga practices, making the form more mindful.
